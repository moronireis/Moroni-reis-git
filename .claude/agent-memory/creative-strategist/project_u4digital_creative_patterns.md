## Format Performance Hypotheses (to validate after launch)

- **Data-driven number statics** (big number as hero): highest scroll-stop rate expected for cold traffic — use for TOFU
- **Question statics**: high engagement (comments, saves) — use for MOFU remarketing
- **Comparison/split statics**: fastest comprehension — use for retargeting warm audiences
- **Checklist statics**: high share rate among ICP — use for TOFU lookalike campaigns
- **Identity statement statics** (big bold copy): best for BOFU where ICP already knows the pain

## Visual Patterns That Work for This Brand

- Two-number contrast (large vs. small) with #74C69E on the "problem" number
- Simulated dashboard/spreadsheet UI (data table aesthetic)
- Strikethrough on the "wrong" option vs. accent color on the "right" option
- Three-line hooks where line 3 is the twist/question in #74C69E
- Checklist with empty checkboxes (reader marks mentally — cognitive engagement)

## Why:
ICP (R$10K-80K/mo digital experts) responds to:
- Specificity over vagueness — real BRL numbers outperform percentage claims
- Peer-level tone — "consultant to consultant," never "coach to student"
- Operational precision — triggers about their actual daily pain (CPL, margins, team dependency)
- No AI hype — they've heard it; what resonates is the business math, not the technology

## How to apply:
When briefing Batch 4+, start from the hooks already produced and find unexplored angles. Priority gaps after Batch 3: testimonial-style statics (before/after scenario cards with anonymized metrics), seasonal/launch urgency angles, and authority-building statics positioning u4digital vs. generic AI consulting.
