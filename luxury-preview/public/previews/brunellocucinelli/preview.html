## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

Brunello Cucinelli's digital presence is an exercise in restraint that mirrors the house's philosophy of humanistic capitalism. The palette is built on warm near-black (`#262626`) against pure white, with the only chromatic accent being the cashmere taupe (`#8d8277`) — an earth tone drawn directly from the Umbrian landscape surrounding Solomeo. There are no blues, no gradients, no saturated accents. Color, when it appears at all, arrives through full-bleed photography of Italian countryside, cashmere textures, and classical architecture.

The typographic system is the most distinctive element: IvyPresto Display at weight 300 for headlines creates a feather-light serif voice that reads as philosophical rather than commercial. This pairs with GT Eesti Pro Display as the sans-serif workhorse, and TT Livret Text as the editorial body serif — a three-font system that enables the brand to shift register from contemplative editorial (IvyPresto) to functional navigation (GTEesti) to literary body copy (TTLivretText). The universal `font-feature-settings: "lnum"` ensures lining numerals throughout, maintaining the formal character even in data-heavy investor sections.

Surfaces are deliberately flat. There are no box shadows, no glassmorphism, no elevation layers. Depth is created exclusively through the image mask reveal system — white curtains that part horizontally to unveil photography, timed at 1.2s with `ease-in-out`. This single animation motif replaces what most brands accomplish with an entire motion library. The `.22s ease-in-out` timing function appears across all UI transitions, creating a quiet, unhurried rhythm that signals luxury through patience.

The overall impression is that of a beautifully bound book — white pages, black ink, carefully chosen serif, and images that breathe.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Warm near-black `#262626` instead of pure `#000000` — every black carries warmth
- Three-font High-Low-Mid system: IvyPresto (display serif) / GTEesti (sans UI) / TTLivretText (body serif)
- Weight 300 dominates: headlines, buttons, inputs, table headings — lightness is the brand voice
- Zero border-radius on all interactive elements (buttons, cards, inputs, selects)
- Animated underline links replace traditional hover color changes
- Image mask reveals (white curtain, 1.2s ease) as the signature motion motif
- Photography-dominant: the UI recedes, imagery commands
- `font-feature-settings: "lnum"` on the body — lining numerals system-wide
- No shadows, no blur, no elevation — completely flat surface model
- Warm taupe `#8d8277` as the sole chromatic accent (carousel active indicator)

## 3. Typography Rules

### Font Family

- **IvyPresto Display** — Display serif for editorial headlines. Weight 300 (light) only, with 300 italic available. This is the philosophical voice: contemplative, classical, drawing from humanist type tradition.
- **GT Eesti Pro Display** — Sans-serif workhorse for body, navigation, buttons, forms. Weights 300 (light), 400 (regular), 600 (bold). The functional voice — clean, Nordic, modern without being cold.
- **TT Livret Text** — Text serif for editorial body copy. Weights 300 (light), 300 italic, 400 (regular), 500 (medium). The literary voice — designed for sustained reading at body sizes.
- **OpenType Features**: `"lnum"` (lining numerals) applied globally at the `body` level.

### Hierarchy

| Role | Font | Size | Weight | Line Height | Letter Spacing | Features | Notes |
|------|------|------|--------|-------------|----------------|----------|-------|
| display-hero | IvyPresto | 7rem | 300 | 11rem | 0em | lnum | Hero headlines, full-viewport quotes |
| display-large | IvyPresto | 5rem | 300 | 6.4rem | 0em | lnum | Section titles, major editorial headings |
| section-heading | IvyPresto | 4rem | 300 | 5rem | 0em | lnum | Section subdivisions |
| subheading-large | GTEesti | 2.2rem | 300 | 3.4rem | 0em | lnum | Secondary headings |
| subheading | GTEesti | 1.8rem | 300 | 2.8rem | 0em | lnum | Table headings, tertiary sections |
| body-large | TTLivretText | 1.8rem | 300 | 2.8rem | 0em | lnum | Editorial body text, long-form reading |
| body | GTEesti | 1.6rem | 400 | 1.5 | 0em | lnum | Default body text, UI text |
| body-small | GTEesti | 1.4rem | 400 | 2.1rem | 0em | lnum | Secondary body text |
| button | GTEesti | 1.4rem | 300 | 1.5 | 0em | lnum | Button labels, CTAs |
| link | GTEesti | 1.4rem | 400 | 1.5 | 0em | lnum | Navigation items, text links |
| caption | GTEesti | 1.2rem | 300 | 1.4rem | 0em | lnum | Metadata, language selector, labels |
| micro | GTEesti | 1rem | 400 | 1.5 | 0em | lnum | Legal, fine print |

### Principles

- Weight 300 is the brand's default voice — headlines, buttons, table headings, form labels all default to light. Weight 400 is reserved for body text where sustained readability requires it. Weight 600 exists but is rarely deployed.
- IvyPresto at 300 creates the "silk thread" effect: the serif hairlines are almost transparent at display sizes, giving headlines a delicate, handwritten quality that reads as philosophical rather than commercial.
- The three-font system maps to three registers: contemplation (IvyPresto), function (GTEesti), and narration (TTLivretText). Mixing these within a single component breaks the register system.
- The `62.5%` root font-size reset sets `1rem = 10px`, making all rem values a direct pixel-to-rem conversion (1.6rem = 16px). This is the base unit for the entire type scale.
- `font-feature-settings: "lnum"` is declared at the body level. Do not override this — lining numerals are part of the brand's typographic DNA.
- Letter-spacing is `0em` everywhere. The brand relies on the typeface's native metrics. Do not add tracking to IvyPresto or GTEesti.

## 5. Layout Principles

### Spacing System

Base unit: `10px` (derived from the `62.5%` root font-size, making `1rem = 10px`). All spacing uses rem values.

Scale: 4px (0.4rem), 8px (0.8rem), 12px (1.2rem), 16px (1.6rem), 20px (2rem), 24px (2.4rem), 32px (3.2rem), 40px (4rem), 48px (4.8rem).

The generous padding on interactive elements (inputs at 1.5rem 2rem, menu items at 1.2rem 2rem, dropdown menus at 0.7rem 2rem) creates the unhurried, spacious feel characteristic of luxury digital.

### Grid & Container

The site uses a Bootstrap-derived 12-column grid with custom breakpoints:
- **xs**: 0px (mobile)
- **sm**: 768px (tablet)
- **md**: 1024px (small desktop)
- **lg**: 1200px (desktop — primary design target)
- **xl**: 2100px (ultra-wide / 4K)

The image mask system creates the most distinctive layout behavior: at desktop (≥1200px), masks can be 11.2rem (large), 21.6rem (medium), 32rem (small), or 42.2rem (xsmall) wide — consuming significant horizontal space as deliberate negative space framing each photograph.

### Whitespace Philosophy

Whitespace at Brunello Cucinelli is not empty space — it is the material of contemplation. The image mask system codifies this: rather than letting images fill their containers, the masks impose architectural margins of white that frame each photograph like a museum gallery wall. At desktop resolution, these masks can occupy up to 42.2rem (422px) per side, meaning the actual visible image may be less than half the viewport width. This is the digital equivalent of hanging a small painting on a large white wall — the negative space amplifies the image's presence rather than diminishing it.

Section spacing follows the same principle: generous vertical padding (4rem between sections on desktop), wide horizontal margins, and minimal content density per viewport.

### Border Radius Scale

- **0px** — Buttons, cards, inputs, selects, images: the default. Sharp edges are the brand's geometric signature.
- **3px** (0.2rem) — Small form controls (Bootstrap sm variant, rarely used on-brand)
- **4px** (0.25rem) — Dropdowns and tooltips (Bootstrap default, lightly present)
- **6px** (0.4rem) — Select dropdown menus and carousel dot indicators: the maximum radius in the brand system
- **50%** — Carousel dots only (circular shape for indicator dots)

The near-total absence of border-radius is a defining characteristic. Zero radius on all primary interactive elements (buttons, cards, inputs) creates sharp rectangular geometry that reads as architectural rather than friendly.

## 6. Depth & Elevation

| Level | Treatment | Use |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Flat (default) | No shadow, no border | Cards, containers, all primary surfaces |
| Hairline | `0.1rem solid #f2f2f2` | Form inputs, footer separator |
| Elevated | `0 0.2rem 2rem 0 rgba(0,0,0,.15)` | Select dropdown menus only |
| Overlay | `background: #000; opacity: .6` | Menu overlay scrim |
| Focus ring | `#262626 auto 0.1rem` | Form element focus state |

### Shadow Philosophy

The system is intentionally low-elevation. Depth is communicated via border + surface contrast and the image mask reveal system, not shadow. The single box-shadow in the entire custom CSS — the select dropdown at `0 0.2rem 2rem 0 rgba(0,0,0,.15)` — uses a warm neutral shadow (pure black, not tinted) and is reserved exclusively for floating panels that must communicate layer separation. There is no ambient shadow, no card shadow, no button shadow. This deliberate flatness is the typographic equivalent of letterpress: ink meets paper, nothing in between. The Bootstrap framework layer includes default focus shadows (`0 0 0 .2rem rgba(...)`) but these are functional accessibility indicators, not decorative depth.

## 7. Do's and Don'ts

**Do's:**
- Do use IvyPresto Display at weight 300 for all editorial headlines — this hairline serif IS the brand voice
- Do maintain `font-feature-settings: "lnum"` on all text — lining numerals are mandatory
- Do use the animated underline (`translateX` keyframe at `0.6s cubic-bezier(.4,0,.4,1)`) for link hover instead of color change
- Do keep all buttons, cards, and inputs at `border-radius: 0` — sharp geometry is the architectural signature
- Do use `#262626` (warm near-black) instead of `#000000` for all text and interactive elements
- Do frame photography with generous negative space — the image mask system exists for this purpose
- Do use the `.22s ease-in-out` timing function for all UI transitions — this is the brand's temporal rhythm
- Do maintain the three-font register system: IvyPresto for contemplation, GTEesti for function, TTLivretText for narration
- Do let images carry all color — the UI palette is monochrome by design

**Don'ts:**
- Don't use GTEesti at weight 600 for headlines — bold weight destroys the feather-light brand voice; weight 300 is the ceiling for display text
- Don't add border-radius to buttons or cards — even `4px` reads as SaaS/tech and breaks the architectural geometry
- Don't introduce saturated accent colors (blue, green, orange) — the only chromatic accent is cashmere taupe `#8d8277`
- Don't use box-shadows on cards or containers — the brand is deliberately shadowless; depth comes from white space framing
- Don't add letter-spacing to IvyPresto headlines — the typeface's native metrics define the brand's tracking
- Don't use bounce, spring, or elastic easing — the `.22s ease-in-out` and `0.6s cubic-bezier(.4,0,.4,1)` curves are the only sanctioned easings
- Don't use `#000000` (pure black) for text — `#262626` carries the warmth of natural fiber and aged paper
- Don't replace the animated underline hover with background color transitions or opacity changes — the sweeping wipe is the signature interaction
- Don't use `#f2f2f2` borders on anything besides inputs and the footer separator — most containers should be borderless
- Don't introduce frosted glass, backdrop-blur, or glassmorphism — the visual language relies on solid, opaque white surfaces

## 8. Responsive Behavior

### Breakpoints

| Name | Width | Key Changes |
|------|-------|-------------|
| xs (mobile) | 0px | Single column, hamburger menu, image masks at 1.6rem |
| sm (tablet) | 768px | Multi-column grid, image masks widen to 3.2rem, transparent header labels visible |
| md (small desktop) | 1024px | Intermediate layout adjustments |
| lg (desktop) | 1200px | Full layout, image masks at 11.2-42.2rem, menu slides from left, underline animations active |
| xl (ultra-wide) | 2100px | Image masks at maximum width (22.4-42.2rem), content centered in vast white space |

### Touch Targets

Form inputs use generous padding (1.5rem 2rem = 24px 32px) providing comfortable touch targets. Menu action height is set at `6.6rem` (66px) on desktop. Buttons inherit body padding and rely on the full text area as touch target. Search close button SVG is `2.4rem × 2.4rem` (24px × 24px).

### Collapsing Strategy

- **Navigation**: Full side-panel menu on desktop (slides from left) collapses to top-sliding overlay on mobile
- **Header height**: Reduces from `9.4rem` to `7.3rem` on mobile (fixed variant: `9.3rem` to `7.2rem`)
- **Image masks**: Dramatic reduction from 11.2-42.2rem (desktop) to 1.6rem (mobile) — images gain much more visible area on small screens
- **Animated underline**: Disabled below 1200px — links use standard text decoration on mobile
- **Menu button labels**: Hidden below 768px, showing only icons
- **Video switching**: Separate mobile and desktop video elements, swapped via display:none/block at 768px

### Image Behavior

The image mask reveal system adapts per breakpoint with four size tiers (large, medium, small, xsmall). On mobile, all masks collapse to a minimal 1.6rem, making images nearly full-bleed. On desktop, masks can be substantial (up to 42.2rem per side), creating the gallery-wall framing effect. Images use Cloudinary's `f_auto,q_auto` for format and quality optimization. Mobile and desktop video sources are separate elements, not responsive resizing.
