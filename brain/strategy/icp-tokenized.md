### Systems: Enterprise Decision Maker

- **pillar**: Systems
- **segment**: Enterprise companies (R$50M+ revenue)
- **demographics**:
  - role: CEO, COO, VP of Operations, Head of Digital Transformation, CTO (business-facing)
  - company-size: R$50M–500M revenue / 200–2,000 employees
  - industry: Financial services, retail, logistics, education, healthcare, agribusiness, professional services
  - geography: Brazil (SP, RJ, BH, Curitiba primary) / LATAM expansion
  - age-range: 38–58
- **psychographics**:
  - mindset: Sees AI as a strategic priority but is skeptical of vendors who overpromise. Has been burned before by tech projects that didn't deliver.
  - sophistication: Business-first — understands outcomes, not architecture. Has a CTO who handles technical vetting.
  - decision-style: Committee-driven with final authority on budget. Needs internal champions. Procurement involved for contracts above R$50K.
- **pains** (top 5, specific):
  1. Approved an AI budget 12–18 months ago and still can't point to measurable ROI — board is asking questions
  2. Operations are not scaling: headcount grows faster than revenue, and margins erode each quarter
  3. Has a backlog of AI "pilots" that never made it to production — the Prototype Graveyard is real
  4. Sales team is expensive but conversion is flat — AI could help but nobody has shown how to make it work inside the existing CRM
  5. Compliance and data governance fears block AI adoption internally — needs a partner who understands the constraints
- **desires** (top 5, specific):
  1. A documented case where an AI implementation generated measurable revenue with a specific number — not "efficiency gains"
  2. A strategic partner who starts with business outcomes, not technology selections
  3. Implementation that integrates with existing systems (Salesforce, HubSpot, SAP, WhatsApp Business API) without a year-long IT project
  4. A methodology they can show the board — structured, traceable, not a black box
  5. Proof that the system will still work 6 months after delivery, not just at the demo
- **objections** (top 5, specific):
  1. "Our IT team says this would take 18 months to integrate with our systems."
  2. "We tried something similar with [Big Agency Name] and spent R$300K with nothing to show for it."
  3. "I need to see a case study from our industry before I can bring this to the board."
  4. "How do you handle data privacy? We're regulated and can't send customer data to third-party APIs."
  5. "What's the maintenance model after delivery? We don't have capacity to manage this internally."
- **voice-of-customer**: "Preciso de resultado mensurável, não de demo bonito." / "Já fomos prometidos mil vezes que IA ia mudar tudo. Quero ver número real." / "O problema não é o investimento — é o risco de investir e não ter retorno de novo."
- **where-to-find**: Executive events (GSEA, HSM, Expo Management), LinkedIn (professional content + event networking), industry association events, CEO peer groups and masterminds, Forbes BR / Exame / Valor Econômico
- **ticket-range**: R$30K–150K per project / potential retainer R$10K–30K/month
- **decision-cycle**: 60–120 days (longer if procurement involved)
- **trigger-events**: Board pressure on AI ROI / Key competitor deploys AI with visible market impact / C-level change (new COO/CEO wants quick wins) / Failed internal AI initiative — needs external help to rescue
- **hormozi-angles**:
  - more-good: "Primeira implementação de IA que o board consegue ver no P&L — com número real, não apresentação de PowerPoint."
  - less-bad: "Parar de explicar para investidores por que R$500K em IA ainda não gerou R$1 de retorno documentado."
  - more-good-others: "Ser o CEO que transformou IA de centro de custo em motor de receita — antes dos concorrentes."
  - less-bad-others: "Eliminar o risco de ser o executivo associado ao projeto de IA que falhou — de novo."

### Systems: Mid-Market Business Owner

- **pillar**: Systems
- **segment**: Mid-market companies (R$1M–50M revenue)
- **demographics**:
  - role: CEO, Founder, COO, Dono de empresa
  - company-size: R$1M–50M revenue / 10–200 employees
  - industry: SaaS, professional services, e-commerce, education, real estate, health and wellness, legal, accounting
  - geography: Brazil (all states, concentrated in SP, SC, RS, MG)
  - age-range: 30–52
- **psychographics**:
  - mindset: Revenue-driven, pragmatic. Has heard about AI but doesn't know where to start without wasting money. Trusts their gut and peer recommendations.
  - sophistication: Business-savvy, tech-adjacent. Uses HubSpot or RD Station, has WhatsApp Business, knows what Make.com is.
  - decision-style: Founder decides, often alone. Fast when convinced. Needs clear ROI case. Budget authority is personal.
- **pains** (top 5, specific):
  1. Sales team drowning in unqualified leads — spending 80% of time on prospects who will never buy
  2. Customer support overwhelmed — same 10 questions answered 50 times a day, creating churn through slow response
  3. Operations held together with duct tape: Notion, WhatsApp groups, spreadsheets, and prayer
  4. Tried Make.com / Zapier automations but they break constantly and nobody knows how to fix them
  5. Growing but margins shrinking — adding headcount is the only lever they know and it's unsustainable
- **desires** (top 5, specific):
  1. An AI agent that qualifies leads 24/7 on WhatsApp and passes only hot prospects to the sales team
  2. Customer support that responds instantly, resolves 70% of tickets automatically, and escalates the rest with full context
  3. A system that actually integrates with what they already use — not another tool to manage
  4. See a pilot working in 30 days, not 6 months
  5. A partner who stays accountable after delivery — not a vendor who disappears
- **objections** (top 5, specific):
  1. "Já tentei automação antes e quebrou na primeira semana sem que ninguém soubesse consertar."
  2. "R$10K é muito — preciso ter certeza que vai pagar em 60 dias ou não consigo aprovar internamente."
  3. "Meu time não é técnico. Quem vai gerenciar isso depois que vocês entregarem?"
  4. "Os dados dos meus clientes ficam onde? Não posso mandar tudo para fora."
  5. "Tenho muito coisa acontecendo agora. Esse projeto vai demandar muito do meu tempo?"
- **voice-of-customer**: "Quero parar de ser o gargalo da minha empresa." / "Meu time passa metade do dia respondendo WhatsApp que podia ser automatizado." / "Se funcionar como você está descrevendo, assino hoje."
- **where-to-find**: LinkedIn (business content), Instagram Reels (entrepreneurship content), WhatsApp business communities, local entrepreneur events, podcasts sobre crescimento de negócios (Shark Tank BR, Primo Rico style), Hotmart Summit, RD Summit
- **ticket-range**: R$5K–20K per project / R$1K–3K/month for retainer/maintenance
- **decision-cycle**: 14–45 days
- **trigger-events**: Lost a key employee who handled manual processes / Competitor gained visible market share / Support crisis (churn spike) / Revenue plateau after headcount growth / Peer in mastermind closed AI deal and saw immediate results
- **hormozi-angles**:
  - more-good: "Um agente de IA que qualifica seus leads no WhatsApp 24/7 e passa só quem vai comprar pro seu time de vendas — sem contratar mais ninguém."
  - less-bad: "Parar de pagar salário para uma equipe que passa o dia respondendo as mesmas 10 perguntas que uma IA pode responder melhor e mais rápido."
  - more-good-others: "Sua empresa funcionando mesmo quando você não está — clientes atendidos, leads qualificados, operação rodando."
  - less-bad-others: "Acabar com o risco de perder clientes por demora no atendimento enquanto concorrentes respondem em segundos."

- **demographics**:

  - geography: Brazil (all states)
  - age-range: 25–50

  - sophistication: Non-technical. Comfortable with smartphone tools. Has tried ChatGPT casually. Does not know the difference between an API and a webhook.
  - decision-style: Emotional + practical. Buys when they trust the person and the ROI case is undeniable. Single decision-maker (themselves).
- **pains** (top 5, specific):
  1. Answering the same 20 WhatsApp messages every day — hours lost that could be billed or used for growth
  2. Missing leads because they don't respond fast enough — someone else gets the client
  3. No system: everything lives in their head and their phone, making vacation or sick days impossible
  4. Cannot afford to hire — need the leverage of a team without the payroll
  5. Spending time on tasks below their hourly rate (scheduling, follow-ups, invoicing, FAQ responses)
- **desires** (top 5, specific):
  1. A WhatsApp bot that handles the first contact, qualifies the lead, and books the meeting automatically
  2. Automated follow-up that nurtures leads they don't have time to chase manually
  3. Something that works without them having to learn to code or manage a tech stack
  4. See it working in a week, not a month
  5. Pay for results, not just the implementation
- **objections** (top 5, specific):
  1. "Isso é muito caro para o meu momento. Não sei se consigo recuperar em 30 dias."
  2. "Nunca usei nada assim. Vou conseguir usar sem saber programar?"
  3. "E se quebrar? Quem cuida disso quando você não estiver disponível?"
  4. "Já vi muita promessa de automação que não funcionou na prática."
  5. "Meu negócio é muito específico — acho que IA não consegue entender meu cliente."
- **voice-of-customer**: "Sou só eu. Não tenho como atender tudo." / "Se eu pudesse clonar eu mesmo, resolvia." / "Preciso de algo simples que funcione."
- **where-to-find**: Instagram (small business content, reels sobre produtividade), YouTube (tutoriais, "como ganhar mais dinheiro"), WhatsApp groups da categoria, local entrepreneur meetups, Hotmart courses community
- **ticket-range**: R$1.500–5.000 per project / [TODO: validate with research — price sensitivity may require entry-level productized offer]
- **decision-cycle**: 3–14 days (fast when convinced, disappears if nurture breaks)
- **trigger-events**: Missed a hot lead due to slow response / Lost a recurring client to a faster competitor / Burned out from doing everything manually / Saw a competitor using automation and felt behind
- **hormozi-angles**:
  - more-good: "Seu WhatsApp respondendo clientes, qualificando leads e agendando reuniões — enquanto você dorme, viaja, ou faz o trabalho que realmente paga."
  - less-bad: "Parar de perder leads bons para quem respondeu 5 minutos antes porque você estava em atendimento."
  - more-good-others: "Seus clientes vendo um negócio profissional e organizado — não uma pessoa correndo atrás de tudo sozinha."

### Builders: Aspiring AI Entrepreneur

- **pillar**: Builders
- **segment**: Professional or entrepreneur who wants to build an AI services business from scratch
- **demographics**:
  - role: Freelancer, mid-level professional (marketing, tech, design, sales), recent grad wanting to start a business
  - company-size: Solo / no business yet
  - industry: Background in marketing, design, tech support, sales, education, HR
  - geography: Brazil (primarily digital-native cities: SP, RJ, Florianópolis, Recife, Curitiba)
  - age-range: 22–38
- **psychographics**:
  - mindset: Hungry. Sees AI as their career leverage point. Wants to stop trading time for money and build something scalable. Follows AI content heavily on YouTube, LinkedIn, Instagram.
  - sophistication: Tech-comfortable but not a developer. Has used no-code tools. Willing to learn. Values community and mentorship over pure content.
  - decision-style: Emotionally driven first (aspiration, identity), then justified logically (ROI, timeline). Peer social proof (seeing someone like them succeed) is the strongest trigger.
- **pains** (top 5, specific):
  1. Has heard about people making money with AI but doesn't know how to structure it as a real business — not a side hustle
  2. Doesn't know how to price AI services without undervaluing or overcharging
  3. No methodology: builds things but doesn't know how to find clients, pitch, or close
  4. Consumed dozens of courses about AI tools but still hasn't made R$1 from it
  5. Imposter syndrome: "Who am I to sell AI to a real business?"
- **desires** (top 5, specific):
  1. Close their first R$5K–10K AI project and have a repeatable process to get the next one
  2. A mentor who has actually built and sold AI projects — not a course creator who teaches theory
  3. A community of people doing the same thing — references, partnerships, and accountability
  4. A sales script and pricing framework they can use immediately
  5. Become the AI expert in their city or niche within 90 days
- **objections** (top 5, specific):
  1. "Já comprei vários cursos de IA e nenhum me ajudou a fechar um cliente."
  2. "Como vou vender algo que eu mesmo não dominei 100%?"
  3. "R$5K–10K de mentoria é muito para quem ainda não está gerando receita."
  4. "E se os clientes não quiserem pagar o que eu preciso cobrar para valer a pena?"
  5. "Tem muita gente vendendo IA agora — como vou me diferenciar?"
- **voice-of-customer**: "Sei que IA é o futuro mas não sei por onde começar a ganhar dinheiro de verdade com isso." / "Quero sair do curso e chegar no projeto real." / "Preciso de alguém que já fez, não de mais teoria."
- **where-to-find**: YouTube (AI tutorials, entrepreneurship content), LinkedIn (first job or side business content), Instagram (motivational + productivity content), Hotmart / Kiwify community pages, Reddit br (empreendedorismo), Discord communities around AI and automation
- **ticket-range**: R$3K–8K for mentorship / R$497–997 for entry-level programs
- **decision-cycle**: 7–30 days (urgency-driven when the right trigger hits)
- **trigger-events**: Saw a peer close an AI project and post about it / Got laid off or income dropped / Consumed content from Moroni or similar creator and felt "this person has cracked the code" / Tired of freelancing for low rates
- **hormozi-angles**:
  - more-good: "Seu primeiro projeto de IA fechado — com cliente real, contrato assinado, e dinheiro na conta — em menos de 90 dias."
  - less-bad: "Parar de estudar IA no modo eterno e nunca cobrar R$1 por isso."
  - more-good-others: "Ser reconhecido como o especialista em IA no seu mercado — antes de todo mundo que ainda está no curso."
  - less-bad-others: "Não ser mais o amigo que 'sabe de IA' mas nunca monetizou — enquanto outros estão faturando."

- **trigger-events**: Lost a client or RFP because competitor offered AI / Existing client asked for AI and they said no / Revenue plateau with no clear next product / Saw a competitor agency rebranding around AI and growing

### Marketing: Business Needing Demand Generation

- **pillar**: Marketing
- **segment**: Established business needing structured marketing and demand generation
- **demographics**:
  - role: CEO, CMO, Marketing Director, Dono de empresa
  - company-size: R$500K–20M revenue / 5–100 employees
  - industry: Professional services, SaaS, education, health and wellness, e-commerce, real estate, B2B services
  - geography: Brazil (all states)
  - age-range: 30–55
- **psychographics**:
  - mindset: Has a product that works but struggles to generate predictable demand. Has tried ads, content, funnels — results are inconsistent. Wants a strategic partner, not an agency that just executes.
  - sophistication: Understands marketing concepts (funnels, CPL, ROAS) but doesn't have in-house expertise to architect and optimize systematically.
  - decision-style: ROI-driven. Will pay for strategy + execution if there's a clear methodology and accountability. Influenced by results and referrals.
- **pains** (top 5, specific):
  1. Marketing spend is unpredictable — some months generate 50 leads, some generate 5, nobody knows why
  2. Agency delivered creatives and ran ads but there was no strategic thinking about funnel, ICP, or positioning
  3. Content is being produced but not converting — lots of engagement, no revenue
  4. Can't measure what's actually driving revenue — attribution is broken
  5. Sales team blames marketing for bad leads; marketing blames sales for poor follow-up — no alignment
- **desires** (top 5, specific):
  1. A demand generation system that produces predictable lead flow — not hope marketing
  2. An AI-powered funnel that qualifies, nurtures, and delivers sales-ready leads automatically
  3. Clear attribution: know exactly which campaign, channel, and message generated each sale
  4. Marketing and sales alignment: one pipeline, one truth
  5. A partner who understands both strategy (what to do) and execution (how to make it work with Meta Ads, email, WhatsApp)
- **objections** (top 5, specific):
  1. "Já contratei 3 agências e nenhuma entregou o que prometeu."
  2. "Quanto tempo leva para ver resultado? Preciso de leads esse mês."
  3. "Como vocês são diferentes de uma agência de performance tradicional?"
  4. "Meu time de vendas não consegue absorver mais leads se eles não forem qualificados."
  5. "O que exatamente vocês fazem que uma agência normal não faz?"
- **voice-of-customer**: "Gasto R$20K/mês em tráfego e não sei o que está funcionando." / "Quero previsibilidade — não depender de mês bom ou mês ruim." / "Meu produto é bom. O problema é aparecer para as pessoas certas."
- **where-to-find**: LinkedIn (marketing and business content), RD Summit, Social Media Week, Meta Ads Manager communities, podcasts sobre marketing e negócios, WhatsApp groups de gestores de tráfego
- **ticket-range**: [TODO: validate with research — REIS IA Marketing pillar ticket not formally defined. Positioning.md shows TBD]
- **decision-cycle**: 30–60 days
- **trigger-events**: Burned through ad budget with no ROI / CMO or marketing manager resigned / New product launch with no proven demand gen system / Investor or board pressuring for growth / Competitor gaining market share through digital channels
- **hormozi-angles**:
  - more-good: "Um sistema de geração de demanda que entrega leads qualificados todo mês — com previsibilidade, não esperança."
  - less-bad: "Parar de queimar budget em tráfego que gera cliques mas não gera receita."
